JFK

John F. Kennedy International AirportNew York City

In 1963, Idlewild Airport was renamed in dedication to John F. Kennedy. Unlike many other airports, it also changed its IATA code from IDL to JFK.

MSY

Louis Armstrong International AirportNew Orleans

In 2001 the airport was named after jazz legend Louis Armstrong, but was previously called Moisant Stock Yards, after aviator John Moisant.

PHX

Sky Harbor International AirportPhoenix

Before the 1930’s, airports had two-letter codes. When codes switched to three letters, many added the letter ‘X’ to the end. Phoenix is one where the ‘X’ makes perfect sense.

ORD

O’Hare International AirportChicago

Before the airport was renamed after Medal of Honor recipient Edward O’Hare in 1949, it was known as Orchard Field Airport and received its IATA code of ORD.

SFO

San Francisco International AirportSan Francisco

Before the 1930’s, airports had two-letter codes. When codes switched to three letters, many added the letter ‘X’ to the end. San Francisco instead used its last letter ‘O’ to make SFO.
